# Israel and Ukraine

I left Israel back in 2015. In total, I had lived there, in my entire life, for several years. My first visit was in 1987 to visit my brother who was doing a year abroad in high school. Then, my second visit was my own year abroad in 1988. After that, my brother and I returned for yet another year in 1991. Fast forward to 1998, we immigrated together. I stayed for about a year and a half before leaving again. And then in 2011, I went back for a few years.

I had seen the country change. A lot. A heck of a lot. Israel was no longer the country I had first visited. It was now radically different. An influx of Russian immigrants during the early 90s made it painfully clear that things were going to change quite drastically.

And, not for the best. For decades, rumours abounded about the Russian mafia in Israel and their ties to politicians such as Netanyahu. The culture corruption was becoming more and more clear. It was, in my eyes, becoming more and more of an international grifter economy in a lot of ways.

This saddened me and filled me with anger. I grew up on the mythology of Israel in my family. Most Jews my age did. A mythology partly based in truth but, also, aggrandisement. I grew up on learning about the brilliance and audacity of the Israeli military as something to admire and be proud of; on the society which made itself from nothing in less than a century.

And I grew up on the mythology that Israel was a free, secular country where Jews could feel at home and safe.

Well, I felt none of that back in 2011 to 2015. I felt only more and more anger about the rise of the ultra-orthodox and the extreme right-wing infested with Russian mob mentality. I never really spoke about why I left, but those things comprised a major part of my leaving - forever, circumstances permitting.

Now we get to the war in Ukraine. Israel has changed completely and is showing its colours. For a country such as Israel not to actually openly and fully support Ukraine during this time is, in my view, tantamount to a betrayal of the very thing which Israel was fighting against for all of these decades. It openly shows the corruption at the very heart which translates into a policy filthy with nothing but self-interest. Gone is the beacon of light which we all looked up to back in the day. In its stead: realpolitik and the willingness to let terrorism destroy an entire country and be "neutral" in a fight with a clear right and wrong side.

It fills me with chagrin to write this, but I will: I no longer support Israel. At all. I don't care for it. I don't want to hear about it. I don't give a damn about it outside of the fact that a lot of my family still lives there. I'm done supporting that place in arguments, and I'm done holding it up for any reason whatsoever. I am disgusted at their lack of support, their "neutrality" and their flimsy excuses.
